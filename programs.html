Warmup

Warming up should be considered before starting any workouts. It's reccomended to warm-up for about 10 minutes. Here's some reccomendations ():

  • jogging on treadmill
  • jump rope
  • walk up and down the stairs
  • jumping jacks

A more detailed warm-up plan will be added in the future!

Beginners Program

Equipment

All you need is just access to a regular gym (LA Fitness, Lifetime Fitness, Snap Fitness, etc etc).

Schedule

Week 1 - Week 16

Day 1(Monday)[Heavy] ⇒ Day 2(Tuesday)[Heavy] ⇒ Day 3(Wednesday)[Active Recovery] ⇒ Day 4(Thursday)[Light] ⇒ Day 5(Friday)[Light] ⇒ Day 6(Saturday)[Active Recovery] ⇒ Day 7(Sunday)[Rest]

After every 4 weeks, I highly encourage you to increase the weights according to the reccomended sets and reps you can perform. For example, on week 4 you can barbell bench 45 lbs without struggling and with perfect form. You can try increasing the weight by 2.5/5/10lbs for safety. Please don't increase it by 50 or 100 lbs at a time. It can lead to serious injury.

Template

Day 1(Monday) and Day 2(Tuesday) are what I like describe as "Heavy" days. They consist of 2 parts:

  1. Strength - Workouts that involves heavier weights you can lift for 5 sets of 4-6 reps, and 1-2 minutes of rest time.
  2. Volume - Workouts that involves lighter weights you can lift for 4 sets of 8-12 reps, and 60-90 seconds of rest time. It targets hypertrophy (increase in muscle size)
Day Muscle Group Target Workout Set(s) Rep(s) Rest
Day 1(Monday) Chest, Shoulders, Triceps Strength Barbell Bench Press 5 4-6 1-2 minutes
Incline Barbell Bench Press 5 4-6 1-2 minutes
Barbell Overhead Press 5 4-6 1-2 minutes
Dumbbell Press 5 4-6 1-2 minutes
Volume Dumbbell Bench or Incline Dumbbell Bench 4 8-12 60-90 seconds
Dumbbell Lateral Raise or Lateral Raise Machine 4 8-12 60-90 seconds
Lying Tricep Extension 4 8-12 60-90 seconds
Day 2(Tuesday) Back, Legs, Biceps Strength Sumo/Conventional Deadlift 5 4-6 1-2 minutes
Barbell Row 5 4-6 1-2 minutes
Squat 5 4-6 1-2 minutes
Dumbbell Front Squat 5 4-6 1-2 minutes
Volume Lat Pulldown 4 8-12 60-90 seconds
Lunges (optional to use weights if bodyweight is too easy/light) 4 8-12 each leg 60-90 seconds
Dumbbell Bicep Curl 4 8-12 each arm 60-90 seconds

Day 4(Thursday) and Day 5(Friday) are what I like describe as "Light" days. They consist of 2 parts:

  1. Volume - Workouts that involves lighter weights you can lift for 4 sets of 8-12 reps, and 60-90 seconds of rest time. It targets hypertrophy (increase in muscle size)
  2. Supersets - A superset is when you perform one set of an exercise and then immediately switch to another exercise and do another set. It involves lighter weights you can lift for 4 sets of 8-12 reps for each exercise, and 1-2 minutes of rest time.

Lateral Raise Machine 5 8-12 60-90 seconds
Superset(s) Dumbbell Bench or Incline Dumbbell Bench and Dumbbell Fly 4 8-12 each workout 1-2 minutes
Dumbbell Press and Dumbbell Lateral Raise or Lateral Raise Machine 4 8-12 each workout 1-2 minutes
Day 5(Friday) Back, Legs, Biceps Volume Seated Row Machine 4 8-12 60-90 seconds
Incline Row Machine 4 8-12 60-90 seconds
45 Degree Leg Press Machine 4 8-12 60-90 seconds
Hack Squat Machine 4 8-12 60-90 seconds
Superset(s) Wide-Grip Pulldown and Reverse-Grip Pulldown 3 8-12 each workout 60-90 seconds
Lunges (optional to use weights if bodyweight is too easy/light) and Dumbbell Squats 3 8-12 each workout 60-90 seconds

Day 3(Wednesday) and Day 6(Thursday) are what I like describe as "Active Recovery" days. It is when you do movements that are less intense than the regular workout days and helps with increasing recovery. Here's some suggestions:

  • Jog/Bike/Swim/Jump Rope for about 30 minutes
  • Foam rolling for 15-20 minutes
  • Yoga - I just find some videos on Youtube and follow along
  • Abdominal workouts

You don't have to do all of them. You can choose 2 or 3 of them.

Day 7(Sunday) is rest day! Yes, resting is a crucial part to getting swole.

What's next after completing this program?

First and foremost, congratulations swoledier! It's one small lift for man, one huge lift for the swole kingdom. Keep up the swolecellent job! At this point, you should be comfortable with most workouts/movements, stronger, and swoler.

What you can do next is either re-do this program with heavier weights and incorporate different workouts/movements or move onto the Intermediate Program.
