Warmup

Warming up should be considered before starting any workouts. It's reccomended to warm-up for about 10 minutes. Here's some reccomendations ():

  • jogging on treadmill
  • jump rope
  • walk up and down the stairs
  • jumping jacks

A more detailed warm-up plan will be added in the future!

Intermediate Program

What's different from Beginner Program?

We will be introducing the following:

  • Progressive Overload - Progressive overload is when you gradually increase the weight, frequency or number of repetitions in your training program.

Equipment

All you need is just access to a regular gym (LA Fitness, Lifetime Fitness, Snap Fitness, etc etc).

Schedule

Week 1 - Week 4

Example: [Barbell Bench 135lbs, 5 sets for 5 reps (5x5)], [Squat 135lbs, 5x5], [Deadlift, 135lbs, 5x5]

Week 5 - Week 8

Increase the weights by about 10%

Example: [Barbell Bench 149lbs, 5 sets for 5 reps (5x5)], [Squat 149lbs, 5x5], [Deadlift, 149lbs, 5x5]

Week 9 - Week 12

Increase the weights by about 10%

Example: [Barbell Bench 164lbs, 5 sets for 5 reps (5x5)], [Squat 164lbs, 5x5], [Deadlift, 164lbs, 5x5]

Week 13 - Week 16

Increase the weights by about 10%

Example: [Barbell Bench 170lbs, 5 sets for 5 reps (5x5)], [Squat 170lbs, 5x5], [Deadlift, 170lbs, 5x5]

Day 1(Monday)[Heavy] ⇒ Day 2(Tuesday)[Heavy] ⇒ Day 3(Wednesday)[Active Recovery] ⇒ Day 4(Thursday)[Light] ⇒ Day 5(Friday)[Light] ⇒ Day 6(Saturday)[Active Recovery] ⇒ Day 7(Sunday)[Rest]

After every 4 weeks, I highly encourage you to increase the weights according to the reccomended sets and reps you can perform. This can apply to all workouts other than the ones mentioned in the example above. Please don't increase it by 50% or 100% at a time. It can lead to serious injury.

Template

Day 1(Monday) and Day 2(Tuesday) are what I like describe as "Heavy" days. They consist of 2 parts:

  1. Strength - Workouts that involves heavier weights you can lift for 5 sets of 3-5 reps, and 1-2 minutes of rest time.
  2. Volume - Workouts that involves lighter weights you can lift for 4 sets of 8-12 reps, and 60-90 seconds of rest time. It targets hypertrophy (increase in muscle size)

Day 1(Monday) Chest, Shoulders, Triceps Strength Barbell Bench Press 5 4-6 1-2 minutes
Incline Barbell Bench Press 5 4-6 1-2 minutes
Barbell Overhead Press 5 4-6 1-2 minutes
Dumbbell Press 5 4-6 1-2 minutes
Volume Dumbbell Bench or Incline Dumbbell Bench 4 8-12 60-90 seconds
Dumbbell Lateral Raise or Lateral Raise Machine 4 8-12 60-90 seconds
Lying Tricep Extension 4 8-12 60-90 seconds
Day 2(Tuesday) Back, Legs, Biceps Strength Sumo/Conventional Deadlift 5 4-6 1-2 minutes
Barbell Row 5 4-6 1-2 minutes
Squat 5 4-6 1-2 minutes
Dumbbell Front Squat 5 4-6 1-2 minutes
Volume Lat Pulldown 4 8-12 60-90 seconds
Lunges (optional to use weights if bodyweight is too easy/light) 4 8-12 each leg 60-90 seconds
Dumbbell Bicep Curl 4 8-12 each arm 60-90 seconds

Day 4(Thursday) and Day 5(Friday) are what I like describe as "Light" days. They consist of 2 parts:

  1. Volume - Workouts that involves lighter weights you can lift for 4 sets of 8-12 reps, and 60-90 seconds of rest time. It targets hypertrophy (increase in muscle size)
  2. Supersets - A superset is when you perform one set of an exercise and then immediately switch to another exercise and do another set. It involves lighter weights you can lift for 4 sets of 8-12 reps for each exercise, and 1-2 minutes of rest time.
